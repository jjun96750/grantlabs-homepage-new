# Grant Labs Platform Playbook

Last generated: 2026-06-23

This file is generated by `npm run content:playbook`. It turns `content-automation/platform-rules.json` into a collaborator-facing posting guide so each channel keeps its own role, format, CTA, and quality checks.

## How To Use

- Start with the campaign folder under `content-automation/output/platform-ready-copy/<date>-<slug>/`.
- Pick the platform-specific ready-copy file listed below.
- Use the matching tracked URL from `content-automation/TRACKED_LINKS.md`.
- After publishing, record the post URL and first signals in `content-automation/PERFORMANCE_LOG.md`.

## Universal Readability Rules

- Every channel must have one clear next action.
- Do not paste Markdown link syntax into editors that do not convert it.
- Keep the raw URL visible on Naver Blog and YouTube pinned comments.
- Keep paragraphs short on Naver and LinkedIn; keep captions short on Reels, Shorts, TikTok, and Instagram.
- Use platform-ready copy files instead of copying from the raw caption pack.

## Naver Blog

- Role: Search-first trust builder
- Best for: Detailed explanations, keyword capture, consultation readiness education
- Format: Long-form post with clear headings, checklist blocks, and one practical example
- Hook: Start with the business owner's immediate risk or missed opportunity
- Asset: One cover image plus 2-3 checklist/table sections
- CTA: Link to the consultation checklist and invite a diagnostic call
- Tone: Specific, practical, and advisor-like
- Avoid: Thin summaries, copied platform captions, exaggerated guarantee language
- Repurpose rule: Use the blog as the source article for all short-form derivatives
- Ready-copy file: `01-naver-blog-copy.txt`

### Publishing QA

- Use the raw checklist URL on its own line.
- Keep headings specific and search-friendly.
- Do not paste Markdown link syntax into the editor.
- Keep paragraphs short enough to scan on mobile.
- Confirm the top CTA and bottom CTA both point to the tracked checklist link.

## Instagram Carousel

- Role: Save-and-share education
- Best for: Step lists, mistakes, before-after readiness, compact visual frameworks
- Format: 7-9 slides with one idea per slide and a save-worthy final checklist
- Hook: Lead with a concrete mistake or surprising eligibility gap
- Asset: Square carousel, bold slide titles, minimal body copy
- CTA: Ask viewers to save the checklist and request diagnosis through the homepage
- Tone: Direct, visual, and concise
- Avoid: Dense legal text, generic motivational copy, unoriginal reposts
- Repurpose rule: Convert blog sections into one carousel sequence
- Ready-copy file: `02-instagram-carousel-caption.txt`

### Publishing QA

- Keep each slide to one idea.
- Make the final slide save-worthy.
- Use the tracked checklist link in profile or story follow-up.
- Avoid long paragraph captions that duplicate the carousel body.
- Make the first slide readable from the profile grid.

## Instagram Reels

- Role: Fast discovery and authority signal
- Best for: Myth-busting, quick diagnostics, founder-facing hooks
- Format: 20-35 second vertical video with captions and a strong first 2 seconds
- Hook: Open with 'If your company is about to apply for funding, check this first'
- Asset: Vertical talking-head or desk scene with burned-in captions
- CTA: Comment or visit the checklist link before applying
- Tone: Sharp, human, and useful
- Avoid: Slow intro logos, overproduced stock clips, no captions
- Repurpose rule: Compress one carousel insight into one reel
- Ready-copy file: `03-instagram-reels-script.txt`

### Publishing QA

- Open with the strongest two-second hook.
- Burn captions into the vertical video.
- Keep the CTA short enough to read on mobile.
- Do not reuse the exact same first frame as YouTube Shorts.
- Use a human, practical tone rather than a report-style script.

## YouTube Shorts

- Role: Quick topic testing and top-of-funnel reach
- Best for: One-question answers, misconceptions, short case breakdowns
- Format: 30-55 second vertical video with title text and spoken answer
- Hook: Ask the exact question the viewer would search or worry about
- Asset: Vertical video, captions, pinned comment pointing to the checklist
- CTA: Watch the related long video or open the consultation checklist
- Tone: Clear, searchable, and answer-first
- Avoid: Pure entertainment without business utility, vague titles, no next step
- Repurpose rule: Turn every blog FAQ into one Short
- Ready-copy file: `04-youtube-shorts-script.txt`

### Publishing QA

- Use a searchable question-style title.
- Pin the tracked checklist link in the first comment.
- Answer one topic only.
- Keep the first sentence understandable without context.
- Avoid vague titles such as 'check this first' without the service keyword.

## YouTube Long-form

- Role: Deep trust and consultation pre-education
- Best for: Step-by-step funding preparation, certification comparisons, case walkthroughs
- Format: 6-10 minute structured explanation with chapters
- Hook: Promise a practical decision framework in the first 15 seconds
- Asset: 16:9 video, chapter markers, pinned checklist link
- CTA: Download or open the checklist before consultation
- Tone: Calm, expert, and structured
- Avoid: Unstructured rambling, unsupported claims, missing chapters
- Repurpose rule: Expand the blog into a teaching script and cut Shorts from each section
- Ready-copy file: `05-youtube-long-description.txt`

### Publishing QA

- Keep chapter markers in the description.
- Pin the tracked checklist link.
- Cut Shorts from each major section after publishing.
- Keep the description structured enough for search.
- Use the blog article as the outline, not as a word-for-word script.

## TikTok

- Role: Pattern interruption and rapid hook testing
- Best for: Contrarian tips, quick audits, relatable founder mistakes
- Format: 15-30 second vertical video with immediate payoff
- Hook: Start with a strong 'Do not apply yet if...' warning
- Asset: Vertical video, captions, visual checklist overlay
- CTA: Ask viewers to check one condition and move to the homepage
- Tone: Fast, plain-spoken, and slightly provocative
- Avoid: Corporate tone, slow pacing, text too small for mobile
- Repurpose rule: Rewrite one Reel into a more direct warning or myth format
- Ready-copy file: `06-tiktok-caption.txt`

### Publishing QA

- Start with the warning or contrarian point.
- Keep captions large and plain.
- Avoid corporate-sounding intros.
- Use one direct problem and one payoff.
- Move quickly to the checklist CTA without sounding like an ad.

## Facebook Page

- Role: Local trust and referral-friendly summary
- Best for: Business-owner explanations, event-style consultation notices, practical summaries
- Format: Short post plus image, with a clear phone/contact CTA
- Hook: Frame the post around a common owner situation
- Asset: Readable image or link preview
- CTA: Call or send a consultation inquiry
- Tone: Friendly, concrete, and local-business oriented
- Avoid: Hashtag-heavy captions, overly technical language
- Repurpose rule: Summarize the blog in 4-5 practical lines
- Ready-copy file: `07-facebook-page-post.txt`

### Publishing QA

- Lead with a common owner situation.
- Include phone/contact CTA when appropriate.
- Use a readable image or link preview.
- Keep the post referral-friendly for business owners.
- Avoid hashtag-heavy endings.

## LinkedIn Page

- Role: Professional credibility and partner trust
- Best for: Policy changes, strategic frameworks, B2B proof of expertise
- Format: Professional text post or document-style carousel
- Hook: Lead with a business implication, not a trend
- Asset: Document carousel or clean text post
- CTA: Invite founders and executives to review readiness before applying
- Tone: Executive, measured, and insight-led
- Avoid: Casual slang, unsupported performance promises, sales-only posts
- Repurpose rule: Turn the blog conclusion into a management insight
- Ready-copy file: `08-linkedin-page-post.txt`

### Publishing QA

- Lead with the business implication.
- Keep the tone executive and evidence-led.
- Avoid casual slang and unsupported performance promises.
- Tie each claim to readiness, sequence, or evidence.
- Use a measured CTA for founders and executives.
